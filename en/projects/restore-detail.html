Architectural Restoration / Bioarchitecture
Ante Operam Post Operam

Summary of the Intervention

The project is based on the dialectic between the preservation of historical memory and the integration of NZEB (Nearly Zero Energy Building) strategies. The goal was to transform a rural building into a high-profile residence, combining the original material envelope with technological inserts in steel, wood, and glass. The design approach focused on enhancing the relationship between interior and exterior spaces, maximizing natural light and views while ensuring high energy performance through a comprehensive retrofit of the building envelope and the integration of renewable energy systems.

Key Points of the Project:

  • External casing: Conservation restoration of the stone walls and internal thermal insulation using high-density wood fiber.
  • Transparency: Installation of large windows with minimal steel frames to maximize the light and landscape views.
  • Energy: Architectural integration of photovoltaic and solar thermal modules in the roof, combined with radiant systems and VMC.

Location Langhe, Piemonte
