Intellectual Journey

A Harmonic Synthesis of
Space, History, and Soul

The professional path of architect Antonino Sinicropi transcends mere construction, evolving into a philosophical and technical inquiry into the quality of living. Rooted in the PAU Department of the Mediterranea University, his work has developed into a holistic vision where heritage restoration and urban regeneration converge toward a single goal.

His connection to historical memory is epitomized by the prestigious intervention at the Cathedral of Gerace. Collaborating with the Diocese of Locri-Gerace and renowned restorer Giuseppe Mantella, Sinicropi explored new frontiers of conservation through 3D surveys: a bridge between traditional craftsmanship and digital precision.

His scientific research for Springer, "Healthy Cities with Healthy Streets", redefines urban infrastructure as a "Third Place." His collaboration with architect Antonio Taccone and his tenure as a Visiting Scholar in Boston have allowed him to adapt international Healthy Streets models into operational strategies.

Influenced by masters like Paolo Pejrone, his approach to greenery is structural: a "cultured garden" where biodiversity integrates with NZEB sustainability. Simultaneously, inspired by the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum, Sinicropi interprets museum spaces as total works of art—a "courtyard of the soul."

This intellectual complexity is further enriched by a profound partnership with architect Antonello Monaco. Within ISAM (Institute for Mediterranean Architecture), their relationship has transcended the academic dimension, evolving into a bond of deep friendship and mutual esteem—an elective affinity between the Roman and Reggina schools of thought that has fostered a symbiotic vision of contemporary design.
